Gemmulem v2.0 — Publication Figures

Thornton, M. & Park, C. (2026). Gemmulem: General Mixed-Family Expectation Maximization with Adaptive Model Selection. University of Texas Southwestern Medical Center.

Figure 1. Computational Speed vs sklearn (2-Gaussian, k=2)
Wall-clock time (ms) for fitting a 2-component Gaussian mixture. Gemmulem (pure C) outperforms sklearn by 1.8–5.1× across all tested sample sizes. Measurements on i9-10850K, WSL Ubuntu 20.04.
Figure 2. Distribution Families by Package
Number of supported univariate distribution families. Gemmulem supports 35 families spanning all parameter spaces; all competitors support ≤5.
Figure 3. k-Selection Method Comparison
BIC score vs. number of components k for a 2-component mixture. All 5 methods correctly minimize at k=2; ICL adds classification entropy penalty that sharpens the minimum.
Figure 4. Adaptive EM: Family Discovery (Mixed N+Γ data)
Log-likelihood convergence for adaptive EM on data drawn from N(−5,1) + Γ(4,2). Each component independently selects the best distribution family, correctly identifying Gaussian + Gamma.
Figure 5. Online EM vs Standard EM — Parameter Recovery
Mean absolute error in component mean recovery across 20 trials. Online EM (mini-batch, Cappé 2009) achieves equivalent accuracy to standard EM while enabling incremental/streaming updates.
Figure 6. EM Convergence — Log-Likelihood vs Iteration
Monotone LL increase per EM iteration for various distribution families. Well-separated data (Gaussian) converges in ~7 iterations; heavier-tailed families (StudentT) converge in ~15.
Figure 7. Spectral Initialization Accuracy
Mean absolute error in initial mean estimates vs. true component means across 100 random seeds, as a function of component separation (σ units). Spectral init outperforms random restart below 4σ separation.
